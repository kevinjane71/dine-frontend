The Biggest Problems Restaurants Face

Before we dive into solutions, let's understand the common pain points that plague restaurants, especially in small and medium cities like Dehradun, Pune, and Chennai.

1. Staff Management Issues

  • High Turnover: Constantly training new staff wastes time and money.
  • Manual Errors: Handwritten orders lead to mistakes, wrong items, and customer complaints.
  • Peak Hour Chaos: During rush hours, staff can't handle the volume efficiently.
  • No Performance Tracking: You can't identify which staff members are most efficient.

2. Billing Errors & Slow Service

  • Calculation Mistakes: Manual billing leads to revenue loss or customer disputes.
  • Long Wait Times: Customers wait too long for bills, reducing table turnover.
  • No Payment Tracking: Cash, card, UPI transactions aren't properly recorded.
  • Tax Compliance Issues: Incorrect GST calculations can lead to penalties.

3. Stock Loss & Inventory Chaos

  • Waste & Theft: No visibility into what's being used vs. what's being wasted.
  • Overstocking: Buying too much leads to spoilage and cash flow problems.
  • Understocking: Running out of popular items during peak hours.
  • No Real-Time Tracking: Manual inventory counts are time-consuming and inaccurate.

4. Menu Management Chaos

  • Outdated Menus: Printed menus become obsolete when prices or items change.
  • No Analytics: You don't know which items are profitable or popular.
  • Inconsistent Pricing: Prices vary between dine-in, takeaway, and delivery.
  • Limited Visibility: Customers can't see your menu online or on their phones.
